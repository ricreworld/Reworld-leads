# Reworld Market Watch — Site Copy

Draft copy for all five territory pages. Written for the reps who use the tool every
morning; search phrases (the terms a rep — or a prospect on the phone — would actually
use) are worked into titles, headings, and opening lines rather than bolted on.

**Site structure (confirmed):** five self-contained territory pages — built as tab
panels in the existing single-page app style: scan sections with per-source status
lines, hand-curated standing-target watchlists, and lead cards with notes, pins, and
pipeline status. No separate services, facilities, or playbook pages — each territory
page carries everything its rep needs, including its own routing and qualification
reference.

**Shared spine on every page (confirmed):**
1. Hottest live leads open the page — freshest, highest-scored signals at top.
2. A routing box on every lead card — where the load can actually go, before the call.
3. Proof = facility hard numbers + the compliance paper trail (COD, chain-of-custody, CEMS).
4. Primary action = **Call now**. Everything else (notes, follow-ups, Salesforce) supports the call.

## Shared Components (appear on every territory page)

These blocks repeat on all five pages. Copy is written once here; each page section
below only lists what's unique to that territory.

### Lead card

- **Score badge:** `HOT 85` / `WARM 62` / `COOL 34` — tooltip: *"0–100. Recency,
  quantity, deal value, service fit, haul distance, customer type. Under 7 days old
  scores hottest — stale records are filtered out before you see them."*
- **Routing box (on every card):** `Routes to: [Facility] — [XX] mi` with a one-line
  fit note, e.g. *"Hempstead — profiled non-haz, drums OK, Part 364 carrier required"*
  or *"Essex can't take this (liquid >1 gal) → Union or partner network."* If nothing
  in-network fits: *"No direct fit — partner network. Note the reason and hold."*
- **Source line:** *"FDA Enforcement — reported [date] — view the government record ↗"*
  Every lead links to its source record, so the signal is verifiable before you dial.
- **Quick actions:** **📞 Call now** (primary) · Google · Email · LinkedIn · Map it
- **Follow-up tracker:** *"Follow-up due [date]"* / overdue: *"Overdue — this lead is
  cooling. Call or kill it."*
- **Status toggles:** New · Called · Qualified · Held · Dead
- **Notes placeholder:** *"What did they say? Who's the contact? What's the volume?
  Future-you is reading this cold."*

### Empty feed state

> **Nothing new in the last scan.** That's real — not a broken feed. Check the scan
> status below, run a Claude Scan to reach sources the direct feeds can't, or work
> your follow-ups: the fastest deal today is usually yesterday's lead.

### Qualification strip (bottom of every feed)

> **Before you migrate to Salesforce, all three must be true:**
> 1. **Worth chasing** — $10,000+ annual opportunity (volume × $/ton, or clear
>    compliance/reputation risk).
> 2. **We can take it** — the waste fits a facility's acceptance list and a service line.
> 3. **Someone to call** — real company, real location, reachable contact.
>
> Unknown quantity? **Hold it and call to confirm** — unknown is not a no.
> Under 1 ton with no compliance risk, expired record, or a known competitor? **Dead.**
> PFAS is the exception: **no minimum quantity, no concentration threshold.**

  Camden added to South Jersey; Essex 2,286 TPD / 66 MW; Union 1,440 TPD / 42 MW; both

  facility as non-DEA pharma only, but earlier material had Union taking DEA loads.

  up. Resolve before build.
- PFAS copy always states: no minimum quantity, no concentration threshold.
- Recall copy always states: ongoing Class I/II only; label-only and archived filtered out.
- Facility staff names appear on routing reference sections only (internal tool);
  never publish PII of lead-site operators anywhere.
- Every lead links to its government source record — the citation is part of the copy.
- Tone: a sharp colleague talking to a rep before their first call, not a brochure.
